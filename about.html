COVID-19 monitor

This dashboard visualises the week-on-week change in coronavirus cases in English local authorities. Further information about coronavirus can be found at: gov.uk/coronavirus

Notes on the data

  • Confirmed cases are recorded up to 9am on the day of publication.
  • The date of confirmed cases refers to the specimen date not the reporting date.
  • The number of confirmed cases is likely to be much lower than the total number of cases because some people are asymptomatic.
  • Cases reported include those identified under pillar 1 (hospital) and pillar 2 (commercial labs and home tests) testing.
  • There is a lag in the data shown because Public Health England state that the most recent cases are 'incomplete'.
  • The data derives from Public Health England's Coronavirus Dashboard and are constantly being reviewed and corrected.
  • Cases reported for Hackney are combined with the City of London and those for Cornwall are combined with the Isles of Scilly.
  • The rate of cases is calculated by dividing the total number of cases by the mid-2020 estimate of the resident population of each local authority multiplied by 100,000.

Further information about the data can be found at coronavirus.data.gov.uk/about

Interpreting the table

  • The table shows the weekly incidence rate for local authorities in England.
  • Local authorities can be sorted by the lowest, highest, or weekly change in incidence rate.

Interpreting the chart

  • The chart shows daily confirmed cases over time.
  • The seven-day average is the average of a day and the three days of confirmed cases before and after it. It is centre aligned because the most recently reported cases are 'incomplete' and therefore likely to change.

Interpreting the map

  • The size of the circles on the map are proportional to the number of confirmed cases reported in each Middle-layer Super Output Area (MSOA) during the latest 7 days of complete data. Each MSOA contains on average 7,200 residents. For more about MSOAs read this helpful blog post by Alasdair Rae.
  • The position of the circle corresponds to the centroid or centre of the MSOA not the location of reported cases.
